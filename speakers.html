Lei Deng

Affiliation: Theory Lab, Huawei

Title: Stabilization control of uncertain networked system with incomplete information

Abstract: Video data accounts for 80% of all Internet traffic, a figure expected to surge further with the rapid adoption of AIGV technology. Efficiently storing, instantly processing, and real-time transmitting massive video data continues to pose significant challenges. This talk will explore two root technologies developed at our theory lab aiming at addressing these critical challenges. One is semantic coding. Different from traditional bit-level video coding such as H.26x, semantic coding focuses on the content and meaning of the video data, ensuring that the most relevant semantic information is preserved and transmitted with priority. The other is real-time communications (RTC). Different from existing best-effort networks, RTC requires that data is transmitted within a given deadline. This fundamentally changes the network design philosophy and thus introduces challenges to redefine network systems. I will both present our theoretical research outputs and practical applications in both directions.

Biography: Dr. Lei Deng received his B.Eng. degree from SJTU, and his Ph.D. degree from CUHK. He was a visiting scholar at Purdue University and was an Assistant Professor at SZU. He joined Huawei Theory Lab in 2022 as a senior researcher. He has published 40+ research papers in top-tier venues, has led and completed one NSFC project as PI, has been granted 10+ patents in both US and China, and has designed 20+ key algorithms that were successfully deployed in Huawei’s products. His current research interest includes semantic coding, RTC, and data science.

Title: 面向边缘端大模型推理的架构和算法研究
